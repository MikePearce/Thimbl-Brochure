Isn't this just like identi.ca/ostatus.net/other open network?

The similarity is only superficial, in that we draw upon similarities with web2.0 platforms in how we communicate our use-case.

Diaspora, Crabgrass, NoseRub, StatusNet, identi.ca and the rest are just web-apps with some sort of federation bolted-on. None of them are truly distributed milti-tier systems like the classic internet applications (email/usenet/irc/finger). Thimbl, on the otherhand, is just a finger & ssh client that illustrates that "microblogging" has already been possible on the internet for decades by simply presenting the data differently and making it look like the now-familiar twitter interface. You don't need anything from the Thimbl project running on your server to participate in Thimbl, just the software that is already in your repository, namely, ssh and xinetd/finger or compatible alternatives.

Like the classic Internet applications, each of Thimbl tiers are themselves distributed, scalable and transparently interoperable with other instances. As a result, Thimbl has far fewer barriers to adoption, it can be hosted cheaply on existing commonly available infrastructure and extends widely available and supported platforms. This also means that Thimbl requires a lot less development than those others.

Another major difference is that those projects are delusional, utopian undertakings that seek to overthrow proprietary platforms by simply writing software, which is why they will remain marginal, used only by niche users.

Thimbl is as much as performance as a platform, it incorporates the critical message of Telekommunisten and doesn't loose track of the fact that Capital is a much bigger factor than software in determining the adoption of a platform. The idea of Thimbl is much more important than Thimbl itself.

What do I need to make this work?

Finger and SSH. Really.

Is it going to work with oStatus?

Yes, we hope so. We want to get the first version done first, which just needs finger and SSH. Then we'll start implementing other methods.

Will Thimbl be an alternative to Twitter?

Well, proprietary, centralized platforms like Twitter are exactly what I'm trying to illustrate are unneeded, a step backward from what we already have in the classic internet platforms, and exist only to capture profit.

While I certainly hope that Thimbl is useful and grows, like all other Telekommunisten "products," it's purpose is primarily symbolic. I'm the last person to argue that all some unfunded collective of activists needs to do to defeat web2.0 is write some nice software. That is simply not the case.

If there can be an alternative to Twitter, a very well funded and positioned corporation, it must be a platform, not simply an alternative service or some clone-ware.

That said, pushing Thimbl as a far as we can is part of the performance, if somehow despite our overt rejection of finance capital, Thimbl explodes beyond it's art/hacker/activist community target audience and becomes more than an artwork, but a legitimate platform, that would be fantastic.

Will you be part of the federated web

Yes, once we get thimbl kickstarted, maybe we can convince all these people that the way forward is not to throw out everything the working internet and it's millions of running servers is actually based on and replace it all from the wire up with something invented yesterday, using code, that if it exists at all, is not part of even a single software repository on the planet anywhere except (maybe) some guy's github account.

Instead, let's use what we currently have and make that more awesome.

Can this approach be used with other social networks?

We'd like to think so, yes. Maybe one day the open web will be all that there is. Time will tell. ;)
